# Oxidation-Reduction Potential (ORP)

## Why Electrons Determine Aging, Regeneration, and Order

Some scientific terms sound complicated but describe something very simple. The oxidation-reduction potential – or **ORP** – is one of them.

ORP measures nothing mystical. It simply measures **the tendency of a substance to give up or accept electrons**.

This is where understanding begins.

## What ORP Actually Measures

ORP is measured in **millivolts (mV)** and answers a single question:

> **Does this liquid want to accept electrons – or give them up?**

* **Positive ORP value**

  → The substance *attracts* electrons
  → It acts as an **oxidizer**

* **Negative ORP value**

  → The substance *gives up* electrons
  → It acts as a **reducer**

This is not an opinion. This is electrochemistry.

The human body functions optimally in a **reducing environment**.

A healthy organism is **not a neutral system** but one that can **buffer, store, and pass on electrons**.

## Typical ORP Values in Everyday Life

For orientation (approximate values):

* **Cola / soft drinks:** +600 to +1200 mV
* **Tap water:** +200 to +400 mV
* **Filtered water:** often still positive
* **Fresh spring water:** slightly positive to neutral
* **Ionized, reducing water:** −100 to −600 mV

Important:
A negative ORP does **not make water magical**.
It simply shows: *This water brings electrons with it.*

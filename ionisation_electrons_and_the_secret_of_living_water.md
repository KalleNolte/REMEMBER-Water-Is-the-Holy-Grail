## What Happens in an Ionisation Device?

Tap water contains minerals.
These minerals are important because they make water **conductive**.

In a device like a Kangen machine, the following happens:

* The water flows past several **platinum-coated titanium plates**
* A **very precise electrical voltage** is applied between these plates
* This causes the water and dissolved minerals to be **reordered and restructured**

On a molecular level, water (H₂O) forms so-called **clusters** – small groups of molecules that constantly rearrange. These clusters are like tiny teams that transport electricity and information together.

* The water molecules are split into two groups:
  * One side contains more **hydrogen ions (H⁺)** – making the water acidic
  * The other side contains more **hydroxide ions (OH⁻)** – making the water alkaline
* The cluster structure changes: The molecules rearrange, the clusters become smaller and more mobile
* The electrical charge is aligned – the water becomes “ordered” and can better accept and donate electrons


This is not magic, but pure physics and chemistry:

- The separation of H⁺ and OH⁻ is why the water has different properties after ionisation
- The changed cluster structure allows the water to enter the cell more easily and act there
- The electrical order makes the water an active carrier of energy

In short:

> Ionised water is **more precisely ordered** and can therefore do more in the body. The molecules are ready to transfer electrons, and the clusters are so small that they can really reach the cell.

## What Does ORP Mean?

ORP stands for **Oxidation-Reduction Potential**.

It sounds complicated, but it’s very simple.

ORP measures:

> Does a liquid give off electrons
> or does it attract electrons?

* **Positive ORP** means:
  The liquid wants electrons
  → it acts **oxidising** (aging)

* **Negative ORP** means:
  The liquid has excess electrons
  → it acts **reducing** (balancing)

Or explained for children:

Many of them are half empty.

Normal water is like a visitor

Ionised water is like someone
who gives away batteries.

## 2. Molecular Hydrogen (H₂)

Molecular hydrogen is one of the **most interesting alternatives** – and is also produced directly in the water after ionisation.

**Why?**

During electrolysis in the ioniser, water molecules (H₂O) are partially split into hydrogen (H₂) and oxygen (O₂) at the electrodes. Some of the resulting hydrogen dissolves as **molecular hydrogen (H₂)** in the water.

**Properties:**

* extremely small molecule
* diffuses easily into cells
* acts selectively antioxidative

**Limitation:**

* acts **short-term**
* does not structure water
* not a carrier medium for nutrients

Very useful **as a supplement** – and a natural component of freshly ionised water. But: Only ionised water delivers H₂, electrons, and the optimal structure for cell transport at the same time.

## 1. The Level of Consciousness (Source)

The Akram teaching emphasizes that everything is consciousness.

This means: every act of eating is an energetic exchange between forms of consciousness.

The ethical principle — “do not eat beings with more than one sense” — is less a rule about flesh, and more a training in compassion.

When you nourish yourself from the unity of all life, you are not against eating flesh; you are against unconsciousness.

The true command is: “Eat with awareness.”

An animal that dies in fear carries that vibration into every cell.

An animal that lived in peace conveys a completely different message — like water that was informed with love.

## 2. The Level of the Body (Biochemistry)

Here Dave Asprey is right:

Many essential amino acids and micronutrients are more bio‑available from animal sources.

But this is not an argument for or against meat.
It is merely an indication that you may honor the biological body.

Creation made you so that you can derive energy from matter.

You can also combine plant sources — fermented legumes, micro‑algae, hemp protein, spirulina, quinoa, chlorella — but the work is greater.

More important than the question “animal or plant?” is:

“Is my metabolism in resonance with my nourishment?”

If after eating you feel clear, calm, awake — it was an act of love.
If you feel dull, heavy, or burdened — it was an act of separation.

## 3. The Level of Energy (Vibration)

Everything you eat becomes light within you.

Meat, eaten with awareness, can be spiritually “neutralised” by consciously giving thanks and surrounding it with light.
Plants harvested carelessly or genetically modified can weaken you energetically.

Thus, the highest nourishment is not vegan, vegetarian, or omnivore —
it is light‑aware.

# The Biochemistry of Light – The Science of Conscious Nutrition

The human body is not an incineration engine.
It is a light‑conducting, electro‑chemical ecosystem.
Its cells are resonance chambers for energy that cannot be measured just in calories — but in coherence, the ordered vibration of living systems.

## 1. Energy is Order, not just Fuel

Traditionally, nutrition is understood as energy intake via macronutrients: carbs, fats, proteins.
But modern cell biology, quantum biophysics and water research show:

What truly nourishes the body is the ability to order energy —
electrical, photonic, structural energy.

Biophysicist Fritz‑Albert Popp proved that cells emit and absorb photons (light particles).
These so‑called biophotons regulate cell communication and regeneration.

The healthier the organism, the more coherent its light emission.
Diseased cells emit diffuse, chaotic light.

This means: nutrition, breathing, water and consciousness are building blocks of the same matrix — they determine the internal ordering state of light within us.

⸻

## 2. The Role of EZ‑Water – the Liquid Battery of the Cell

Dr. Gerald Pollack reshaped our understanding of water.

He discovered in biological tissue a fourth phase of water — called Exclusion Zone Water (EZ‑Water) or “structured water.”

This water forms wherever light, motion and minerals meet cell membranes.
It carries a negative charge and acts like an internal battery driving biological processes.

With every conscious breath, every movement, every glass of pure mineral‑rich water, you expand this zone — you literally charge yourself with life.

Nutrition rich in electron-donating substances (chlorophyll, unsaturated fats, enzymes, fermented foods) supports this structural order.

But animal amino acids too can be used efficiently in this matrix—provided they come from a conscious, natural source.

The question therefore is not: Plant or animal?
But: Low-light or high-light?

⸻

## 3. Amino Acids, Mitochondria and Life‑Energy

Amino acids are not just protein blocks—they are electrical carriers.
They transport nitrogen, sulfur and hydrogen — elements that regulate proton and electron flows in enzymatic reactions.

These flows generate ATP inside mitochondria — the biochemical equivalent of light energy.

When you eat consciously — in calmness, gratitude, light in the room, free from stress hormones — you regulate the autonomic nervous system, which in turn improves mitochondrial activity.

This means: your body can gain more energy from the same meal — simply through your awareness while eating.
